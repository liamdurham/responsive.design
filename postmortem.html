    WGD242 Post Mortem

WGD 242 Post Mortem

A long week...

In this course, I further learned jQuery html control, as well as methods of combining jQuery and CSS to create animated or responsive behaviors. In addition, full page scrolls are something that I have never before attempted, and thus have been added to my repertoire. I have used many forms, but in-depth form validation is fairly new to me. I appreciate the many things I have discovered in this class.

 

Theme choices

Building blocks?.

My theme choices revolved around a fictional company called Havok Less Than Lethal. For this company, a standard social-esque blue has a doubling effect as a police-support message. My navigation includes a menu system and a sidebar (though I still don't like side bars, I find them clunky). The images in my slider are the business logo I created, a picture of Less than Lethal weapons, and a fictional LTL taser-like underbarrel attachment, which is what this company attempts to sell. Decisions for UX/UI were made with best practice in mind, such as the horizontal "fly-out" mobile menu and at-the-top logo placement. Form responses were chosen in-character as a small but growing company catering mostly to police and government orders.

 

Lab Locations

Slider: Index. Form: Contact. Hidden Image: What is LTL. Full page scroller: Week 5 Lab. Most other labs involve significant parts of the site, including the media responsivness and menu. As I was creating a full website, I did not keep these labs seperate from the main and final project, instead building them in to the website as I went.
